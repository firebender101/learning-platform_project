Top Mental Health Practices for Men

1. Daily Self-Check-In

Take 5 minutes each morning to ask yourself how you feel — physically, mentally, and emotionally. Identify your stressors and focus on what you can control.

2. Physical Activity

Exercise is one of the best ways to boost your mood. Try 30 minutes of walking, cycling, or gym workouts daily.

3. Healthy Sleep Routine

Aim for 7–8 hours of quality sleep. Go to bed and wake up at consistent times.

4. Talk — Don’t Bottle It Up

Open up to someone you trust — a friend, partner, or therapist. Talking reduces loneliness and shame.

5. Practice Mindfulness or Meditation

Use apps like Calm or Headspace. Try deep breathing — inhale 4s, hold 4s, exhale 4s, hold 4s — to calm your mind.

6. Limit Alcohol and Substance Use

Alcohol may feel relaxing, but it’s a depressant. Set limits and find healthy stress relievers instead.

7. Build a Support Network

Stay connected with family and friends. Join a community or group to share and grow together.

8. Seek Professional Help When Needed

Therapy isn’t weakness — it’s strength. Reach out early for help from a counselor or psychologist.

9. Learn to Rest and Recharge

Take breaks, enjoy your hobbies, and unplug from technology sometimes.

10. Gratitude and Positive Reflection

Each evening, note three things you’re grateful for. Gratitude helps shift your mindset toward positivity.
